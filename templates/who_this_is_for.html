Who this is for

Four audiences asking different versions of the same question — "can anyone independently verify what our AI actually did?"

This is an early-stage demo of ario-mlflow, a plugin that anchors signed proofs of training runs, model registrations, and every individual inference to ar.io. Four kinds of people come to this demo for four different reasons — the cards below are each of their doorways in.

ML engineer in a regulated industry
"Which model made this decision 14 months ago?"
"The regulator just asked about a specific credit decision from Q2 last year. The MLflow run was pruned. I can't reconstruct it."
Call ario_mlflow.anchor() once inside your training loop and every run mints a signed proof anchored to ar.io. Even if your MLflow server is gone, any third party can fetch the proof and verify exactly what ran.

Head of AI governance
"What fraction of our AI decisions are actually auditable?"
"Our CRO asks quarterly. Today I have to guess. Different ML teams use different stacks. Nothing is consistent."
Today this demo shows one anchored model; a coverage dashboard and policy gates ("can't promote to prod unless lineage is anchored") are on the roadmap. The proof format is already in shape for consolidated reporting.
See model lineage in action →
External ML auditor
"Is the model they evaluated the model they deployed?"
"Clients hand me Jupyter notebooks and evaluation CSVs. I have no way to verify those artifacts are the ones actually in production."
Every anchored proof is independently verifiable — re-hash the canonical record, verify the signature, fetch the permanent copy from ar.io. No client access required. A standalone verifier portal is on the roadmap.
Verify a sample decision →
Open-source model publisher
"How do I prove the weights match my paper?"
"I published a fine-tune on Hugging Face. Someone claims the weights don't match the results in the paper. I'd like to prove they do."
The proof format (SHA-256 artifact hash + signed commitment anchored to ar.io) is public-model-publishing friendly. A dedicated huggingface-cli plugin and a model-card badge are on the roadmap — the underlying primitive is usable today.
Read the roadmap →
A note on scope. This is an early-stage demonstration of a proof-of-provenance pipeline, not a production compliance tool. The plugin anchors model-artifact hashes and decision records; deeper commitments (training-data hashes, external identity binding, continuous verification, semantic attestation) are explicitly on the roadmap. The goal today is to make the core primitive easy to try and honest about what it covers.
